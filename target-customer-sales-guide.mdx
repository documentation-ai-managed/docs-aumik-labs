## PART 4: SALES PROCESS

### Step 1: Qualify the Lead

**BANT Framework:**
- **Budget:** Can they afford your services? (Min $500/mo US, â‚¹25K/mo India)
- **Authority:** Are you talking to decision maker?
- **Need:** Do they have a real pain point?
- **Timeline:** When do they need to move?

**Qualifying Questions:**
```
1. What's driving your interest in [colocation/dedicated hosting]?
2. What are you currently using for infrastructure?
3. What's your monthly spend on cloud/hosting today?
4. Who else is involved in this decision?
5. What's your ideal timeline to make a change?
```

---

### Step 2: Discovery Call (30 min)

**Agenda:**
1. Understand their current setup (5 min)
2. Identify pain points (10 min)
3. Discuss requirements (10 min)
4. Propose next steps (5 min)

**Key Questions:**
```
Current State:
- Walk me through your current infrastructure setup
- What's working well? What's not?
- What does your cloud bill look like monthly?

Pain Points:
- What would happen if nothing changes in 6 months?
- Have you had any outages or performance issues?
- Are there compliance requirements you're struggling with?

Requirements:
- What does success look like for you?
- What's most important: cost, performance, or support?
- Any specific certifications or compliance needed?
```

---

### Step 3: Proposal

**Proposal Structure:**
1. Executive Summary (their problem, your solution)
2. Proposed Solution (specific configuration)
3. Pricing (clear, no hidden fees)
4. SLAs (uptime, support response)
5. Implementation Timeline
6. Why Us (differentiators)
7. Next Steps

**Pricing Presentation:**
```
Option A: Basic Colocation
- X racks, Y power, Z bandwidth
- $X,XXX/month
- Best for: [use case]

Option B: Managed Hosting (Recommended)
- Everything in A, plus management
- $X,XXX/month (save 20% vs separate)
- Best for: [use case]

Option C: Full Solution
- Everything in B, plus DR site
- $X,XXX/month
- Best for: [use case]
```

---

### Step 4: Close

**Closing Techniques:**

1. **Assumptive Close:**
"So if we can start deployment next Monday, does that work for your team?"

2. **Summary Close:**
"So we've agreed on [configuration], [price], and [timeline]. Ready to move forward?"

3. **Urgency Close:**
"We have 3 racks available at this price. After that, it's a 4-week wait. Want to lock this in?"

4. **Trial Close:**
"How about we start with a 1-month pilot at [reduced price]? If it works, we'll sign the annual."
